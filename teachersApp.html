AI Applications for Teachers

Explore these AI-powered applications designed to help teachers create interactive and engaging lessons, simplify grading, and improve student performance.

ChatGPT (AI Conversational Assistant)

ChatGPT is an advanced AI-powered conversational model developed by OpenAI. It assists in answering questions, solving problems, writing content, and learning new topics. Students, professionals, and enthusiasts use it to explore and understand complex topics.

Try ChatGPT Watch on YouTube
Gamma.app

Gamma App is an innovative tool designed to revolutionize the creation of AI presentations, webpages, and documents. It simplifies the content creation process, allowing users to generate polished content effortlessly without needing to focus on formatting or design.

Try gamma.app Watch on YouTube
EdPuzzle (Interactive Video Learning)

EdPuzzle allows teachers to insert quizzes and questions into educational videos. Teachers can track students' progress and assess their understanding of the content.

Try EdPuzzle Watch on YouTube
Magic School

Magic School is an AI-powered platform that offers tools for content creation, idea generation, and making material accessible. It's a time-saver for teachers!

Try Magic School Watch on YouTube
Slidesgo

Slidesgo provides free templates and an AI-powered Presentation Maker. Create a presentation in minutes by selecting a topic and tone, then editing and downloading your slides.

Try Slidesgo Watch on YouTube
CoGrader

CoGrader uses AI to simplify grading essays, giving teachers more time to focus on teaching and providing meaningful feedback.

Try CoGrader Watch on YouTube
Canva AI-Powered Tools

For those educators who, like many, struggle with design, there's good news. Canva's Docs to Decks tool magically transforms a simple written outline into a series of visually appealing slides. The process is straightforward: submit an outline and choose from the generated slide designs, then customize as needed.


Another gem from Canva is the Magic Design AI tool. Instead of starting with an outline, educators can initiate a complete presentation from a single, well-phrased prompt. The results are nothing short of impressive.
Try Canva Watch on YouTube
